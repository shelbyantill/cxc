This project expanded my skill set in web game development with visual, audio, and physical user interaction. The goal of the game is to navigate a hot air balloon through a sky filled with obstacles in thirty seconds. The player needs to avoid collisions, and the game becomes more difficult over time as the number and speed of obstacles increase. In my public speaking course, I argue that the school system's reliance on grades and standardized tests as measures of success is both ineffective and detrimental to students. Drawing from my personal experience in a competitive magnet school, I discuss how the pressure to perform academically negatively affects students' self-esteem and mental health. I also highlight that grades fail to capture important qualities such as emotional intelligence, creativity, and leadership skills. Citing examples of successful individuals like Steve Jobs and Bill Gates, I emphasize that true success cannot be measured by academic performance alone. In conclusion, I encourage the audience to critically evaluate the grading system and recognize that success extends far beyond test scores.
